Encapsulation (also called information hiding) consists of separating the external aspects of an object, from the internal implementation details of the object, which are hidden from other objects. Encapsulation prevents a program from becoming to interdependent that a small change has massive ripple effects. Encapsulation has ability to combine data structure and behavior in a single entity makes encapsulation cleaner and more powerful than in conventional languages that separate data structure and behavior.
